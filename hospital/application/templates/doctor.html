GENERAL MEDICINE

A team of dedicated Physicians, along with qualified support staff, ensure that every patient who approaches us has their medical needs identified and dealt with. The General Medicine department at BMH collaborates with other specialities in dealing with the diagnosis and treatment of various communicable and non-communicable diseases. To ensure comprehensive treatment to the patients, we have state‐of‐the‐art radio‐diagnosis and laboratories with the most sophisticated equipment. The dedicated and highly skilled doctors at the hospital provides targeted patient care in an environment supported by evidence‐based diagnosis backed by education and research. The department provides efficient support services to other specialities by attending to cross‐ referrals. The hospital has the expertise seasoned through 3 decades of handling a large number of critically ill patients with high standards of clinical care, so you can be assured of being in expert hands. Comprehensive set of preventive health checkups are offered to assess health status and advise preventive measures.

Cardialogist

A cardiologist is a doctor who’s an expert in heart and blood vessel diseases. They can treat heart diseases and help keep you from getting heart diseases. After completing four years of medical school, cardiologists spend three years learning general internal medicine as a resident, plus at least three more years of specialized training after that. After 10 years of training, a cardiologist can take an American Board of Internal Medicine exam. Even after achieving board certification, cardiologists keep learning for as long as they practice. They must keep up with the latest advances in how to treat patients to provide the best care.
